Featured Student Case Study — Spring 2025

Assignment 1: ASCII Art & Video Generation

Submitted by Pranab Kumar Mondal (MSc Computer Science; B2330048)

Exemplary Submission

1. Grey-Level Mapping vs. PCA-Based Mapping

In converting standard images (`monkeyking.jpg`) to ASCII text, a controlled 10-character grey-level mapping (`@%#*+=-:. `) provided superior structural clarity and edge sharpness compared to a 70-character set. While a 70-character set offers finer intensity gradations, the varying physical widths and textures of ASCII symbols introduce visual noise. Furthermore, implementing a Principal Component Analysis (PCA) mapping based on the $L_2$ norm of character patches primarily emphasized image contours and outlines rather than smooth intensity gradients.

2. Video to ASCII Conversion & Parameter Optimization

Converting full video sequences (`bleach_lr.mp4`) required synchronizing frame rate extraction, individual frame mapping, audio re-synthesis, and optimal patch scaling. Evaluating frame widths across 80, 160, and 240 pixels revealed that an output width of 240 pixels achieved the optimal balance between high detail retention and smooth temporal playback.

3. Color ASCII Reconstruction

Attempting to split frames into separate R, G, and B channel ASCII maps resulted in spatial misalignment and severe visual artifacts. The successful approach calculated the average patch color and assigned it directly to the corresponding grayscale symbol, utilizing a reversed 70-character grey mapping over a black background to preserve accurate visual contrast.
